Treat your pets Age seriously

As our furry friends age, their health change just as much as ours do. Dogs and cats are considered "senior" when they reach seven years of age, and from this point on, they require extra attention and care to ensure their well-being. Senior pets are more susceptible to age-related illnesses, such as arthritis, diabetes, and kidney disease, and may require different diets, exercise routines, and even medication to manage their health effectively.

Tick season is here!

With the arrival of spring, tick season is once again upon us. These tiny blood-sucking parasites can cause serious health problems for both humans and pets. While there are several ways to prevent tick bites, such as using tick repellents and avoiding areas with high tick activity, one of the most effective measures is getting your pet vaccinated.
